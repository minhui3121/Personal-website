Medieval architecture

Medieval architecture was a common architectural style during the Middle Ages (5th-15th century). Many religious, civil, and military buildings was affected by this style. However most buildings who haven't been destroyed are churches and castles. The castle presented beside is called Bodiam Castle. Is was built in 14th century and is located in South England. The landscape around the castle inscreases its aesthetic appeal. Sandscript stone was used during the construction and it was build by Sir Edward Dalyngrigge.

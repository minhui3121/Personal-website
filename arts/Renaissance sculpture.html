Renaissance Sculpture

The main elements distinguishing Renaissance sculpture from other sculptures from different time periods were physical realism and classical composition. The sculpture presented beside is called Pieta and was created by Michelangelo in 1498-1499. Michelangelo was a Renaissance italic scultor who made several masterpieces including Pieta. The sculpture represents the form of Jesus and Mary, the Mother of Jesus. The work was done in marble and has the dimension of 174cm x 195cm. The work is now housed in St. Peter's Basilica, Vatican City.
